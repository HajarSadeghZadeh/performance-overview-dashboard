# Performance Overview Dashboard
## Business Requirements Document

### 1. Project Background

During my research and professional work, I worked with hospital operations data from New South Wales, Australia, with a particular focus on surgical activity, operating theatre usage, elective and emergency demand, waiting times, cancellations, and service performance.

A key challenge in this setting was that operational information was often distributed across multiple data sources and reports. Stakeholders needed a clearer way to monitor performance, understand where bottlenecks were occurring, and identify areas that required planning attention.

The main business problem was not simply the lack of data. The problem was that decision-makers needed data to be transformed into a clear reporting view that could support operational monitoring and evidence-based decisions.

This project was developed as a public portfolio version of that type of business intelligence solution. The dashboard is inspired by real hospital reporting needs, but all data used in this version is synthetic and has been generated to reflect realistic operational patterns while protecting confidentiality.

### 2. Project Purpose

The purpose of this dashboard is to provide a single reporting interface where stakeholders can monitor key operational performance indicators, including activity volume, resource utilisation, waiting time, cancellations, emergency demand, and performance trends.

The dashboard is primarily designed for health service stakeholders, including operational managers, performance reporting teams, planning teams, and executive decision-makers. However, the reporting framework is transferable to other operational environments where organisations need to monitor demand, capacity, utilisation, delays, and service performance.

### 3. Stakeholder Needs

The intended stakeholders needed to answer questions such as:

- How many procedures are being completed over time?
- Which departments or service areas are experiencing the highest demand?
- How efficiently are operating rooms or resources being used?
- Where are waiting times increasing?
- What proportion of activity is elective versus emergency?
- What are the main causes of cancellations?
- Which service areas require additional planning attention?

### 4. Public Portfolio Version and Synthetic Data

This GitHub project is a public and fully anonymised portfolio version of the dashboard concept.

No real patient-level data, confidential hospital data, proprietary files, or identifiable information has been used. The dataset has been synthetically generated to preserve the structure and behaviour of realistic operational data.

### 5. Dashboard Pages

The planned dashboard includes:

1. Executive Overview
2. Activity & Utilisation
3. Waiting & Demand Analysis
4. Cancellation Analysis
5. Detailed Records

### 6. Success Criteria

The project will be considered successful if:

- The dashboard clearly communicates key operational KPIs.
- Stakeholders can identify performance trends and bottlenecks quickly.
- The data model follows a professional BI structure.
- DAX measures are clearly defined and documented.
- The GitHub repository is easy to understand and professionally documented.
